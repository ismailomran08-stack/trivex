How Long Does Restaurant Construction Take? Timeline Guide (2026)

By Trivex Group | March 22, 2026 | 5 min read

Whether you're opening a new QSR location or renovating an existing restaurant, timeline is everything. Every week of construction is a week you're paying rent without generating revenue. Knowing what to expect — and what can go wrong — helps you plan smarter and open faster.

Typical Restaurant Construction Timelines

Restaurant construction timelines in Canada vary based on the scope and complexity of the project. Here's what to expect:

  • Small QSR build (under 1,500 sq ft) — 6 to 8 weeks. Juice bars, sub shops, and counter-service concepts with simple kitchen setups.
  • Full-service restaurant (1,500-3,000 sq ft) — 10 to 16 weeks. Full commercial kitchens, dining rooms, bar areas, and complex mechanical systems.
  • Major renovation or rebrand — 4 to 8 weeks. Updating an existing restaurant with new finishes, equipment, and brand identity while preserving structural elements.
  • Quick-turn facelift — 3 to 7 days. Cosmetic refreshes like new paint, signage, flooring, and minor fixture updates.

These timelines assume construction only — they don't include the pre-construction phase of design, permitting, and procurement, which can add several weeks.

Construction Phases and How Long Each Takes

Every restaurant construction project follows a predictable sequence. Understanding each phase helps you track progress and anticipate what's coming:

  • Permitting and approvals (2-4 weeks) — Building permits, health department review, fire safety review, and any zoning approvals. This happens before construction starts, and timelines vary significantly by municipality.
  • Demolition and site prep (3-5 days) — Removing existing finishes, walls, and fixtures. In a shell space, this phase is minimal.
  • Rough-ins: mechanical, electrical, plumbing (2-3 weeks) — Running ductwork, gas lines, plumbing, and electrical wiring before walls are closed. This is the most complex phase and drives the overall timeline.
  • Framing and drywall (1-2 weeks) — Building interior walls, installing drywall, taping and mudding.
  • Finishes (2-3 weeks) — Flooring, paint, tile, millwork, countertops, equipment installation, and signage.
  • Final inspections and punch list (3-5 days) — Building inspection, health inspection, fire inspection, and addressing any deficiencies.

What Causes Delays?

The four most common causes of restaurant construction delays in Canada are:

  1. Permit processing times. Some municipalities take 2 weeks; others take 6+. If you haven't submitted permits before signing your lease, you're already behind.
  2. Supply chain issues. Specialized kitchen equipment, custom millwork, and brand-specific materials can have 4-8 week lead times. Order early.
  3. Scope changes mid-project. Every change — no matter how small — requires repricing, rescheduling, and potentially re-permitting. Lock your scope before construction starts.
  4. Weather (for exterior work). If your project includes patio construction, exterior signage, or facade work, Canadian winters can add weeks to your timeline.

How to Stay on Schedule

The single most effective way to keep your restaurant build on schedule is to work with a contractor who makes decisions fast. In a partner-operated model, the person managing your project is an owner — not a salaried PM who needs to "check with the boss." That means site decisions happen in minutes, not days.

How long does it take to build a QSR restaurant in Canada?

A typical QSR (quick-service restaurant) build in Canada takes 6 to 8 weeks from the start of construction to handover. This assumes permits are already in hand. Add 2-4 weeks for permitting if you haven't started that process yet.

What causes delays in restaurant construction projects?

The most common causes of restaurant construction delays are permit processing times, supply chain issues with specialized kitchen equipment, mid-project scope changes requested by the owner or franchisor, and weather delays for any exterior work. Working with an experienced contractor who manages these risks proactively can keep your project on track.
