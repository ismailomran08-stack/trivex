How Much Does a Franchise Fit-Out Cost in Canada? (2026 Guide)

By Trivex Group | March 22, 2026 | 5 min read

If you're opening a franchise location in Canada, one of the first questions you'll ask is: how much will the fit-out actually cost? The answer depends on several factors, but having a realistic budget from day one can save you months of delays and thousands in unexpected expenses.

Typical Franchise Fit-Out Cost Ranges

In Canada, most QSR (quick-service restaurant) franchise fit-outs fall between $150,000 and $500,000. That's a wide range, and where you land depends on the specifics of your project:

  • Small QSR (under 1,500 sq ft) — $150K to $250K. Think juice bars, sub shops, or small coffee concepts with minimal kitchen requirements.
  • Mid-size QSR (1,500-2,500 sq ft) — $250K to $400K. This covers most pizza, burger, and ethnic food franchise builds with full commercial kitchens.
  • Large or full-service franchise (2,500+ sq ft) — $350K to $500K+. Full dining rooms, complex kitchen exhaust systems, walk-in coolers, and extensive finishes.

What Factors Affect Franchise Fit-Out Cost?

No two franchise builds are identical. Here are the main variables that drive your total cost up or down:

  • Square footage — More space means more materials, more mechanical work, and more labour hours.
  • Brand requirements — Some franchisors have strict millwork, signage, and equipment specs that limit where you can save.
  • Kitchen complexity — A franchise with a full commercial kitchen, hood system, grease trap, and walk-in cooler costs significantly more than a counter-service concept.
  • Location and province — Labour rates and permitting costs vary by province. Ontario and Alberta tend to be higher than Atlantic Canada.
  • Existing conditions — Are you building in a shell space or converting an existing restaurant? A second-generation space can save $30K-$80K.
  • Permits and inspections — Municipal building permits, health department approvals, and fire inspections all carry fees that vary by municipality.

Cost Breakdown: Where Does the Money Go?

A typical franchise fit-out budget breaks down roughly as follows:

  • Demolition and site prep — 5-8% of total budget
  • Mechanical (HVAC, plumbing, gas) — 18-25%
  • Electrical — 12-15%
  • Kitchen equipment and installation — 15-25%
  • Finishes (flooring, paint, millwork, counters) — 15-20%
  • Signage (interior and exterior) — 3-6%
  • Permits, fees, and soft costs — 5-8%

How to Budget Properly

The biggest budgeting mistake franchise owners make is underestimating soft costs and contingency. Here's how to avoid it:

  1. Get a detailed estimate before signing a lease. Your contractor should walk the space and give you a line-item quote, not a ballpark.
  2. Budget 10-15% contingency. Unexpected conditions behind walls, permit delays, and material price changes happen on every project.
  3. Lock in material pricing early. Supply chain volatility can push costs up 5-10% if you delay purchasing.
  4. Coordinate with your franchisor early. Get their equipment specs and approved vendor list before your contractor starts pricing.
